Executive Summary
LMAR Marketing Real Estate
Meta Ads Performance Report
Period: May 13 – June 11, 2026  ·  Prepared by: Nasrat Abid, Meta Ads Specialist  ·  27 Campaigns
1. Overview
During the 30-day period from May 13 to June 11, 2026, LMAR Marketing Real Estate ran 27 Meta Ads campaigns across Facebook and Instagram, with a total spend of ₨49,174.54 PKR. The campaigns collectively generated 1,803,429 results, reached 1,995,867 unique accounts, and delivered 2,447,496 impressions. A total of 172 messaging contacts were made, with 151 being new contacts.
2. Campaign Mix & Budget Allocation
Campaigns were distributed across three objectives:
  • Post Engagement (8 campaigns): ₨15,075 spend — 30.7% of total budget. Generated 183,123 engagement results at an average CPR of ₨0.08–₨0.15.
  • Reach / Awareness (12 campaigns): ₨25,829 spend — 52.5% of total budget. Generated 1,620,182 reach results. The dominant objective this period.
  • Lead Generation (7 campaigns): ₨8,271 spend — 16.8% of total budget. Generated only 124 lead results but produced 151 new messaging contacts.
Of the 27 campaigns, only 2 were active at report time, 20 were inactive, 4 archived, and 1 pending review — indicating most budget was spent earlier in the period.
3. Top Performing Campaigns
🏆 Highest Reach: New Awareness Campaign #4 — 343,922 people reached at ₨7,124 spend. Highest single-campaign reach of the entire period.
⚡ Most Efficient Engagement: New post engagement campaign #8 (Active) — ₨0.0524 per engagement result. Best CPR in the account.
💬 Top Lead Generator: New Leads Campaign Faisal 2 — 108 new messaging contacts from ₨5,101 spend. Generated 71.5% of all new leads.
💰 Best Reach Efficiency (Archived): Archived Awareness #3 achieved ₨5.07 per reach result — the lowest CPR among all awareness campaigns.
4. Period-over-Period Comparison (vs Apr 11 – May 10)
  • Spend decreased by 48.4% — from ₨95,178 to ₨49,175. Significantly lower budget this period.
  • Results increased by 46.2% — from 1,233,662 to 1,803,429 despite lower spend. Strong efficiency improvement.
  • Reach held steady — 1.99M vs 2.05M (−2.7%). Nearly same reach at half the cost.
  • Campaigns increased — from 17 to 27 campaigns (+10). More diversified campaign structure.
  • Impressions slightly down — 2.45M vs 2.73M (−10.2%). Fewer impressions but more results suggests better targeting.
Overall, this period demonstrates significantly better cost efficiency — achieving more results with nearly half the budget compared to the previous period.
5. Areas of Concern
  • Lead campaign CPR is very high: Most lead campaigns spent ₨200–₨321 per result with only 1–8 conversions. New Engagement Campaign F/T 2 spent ₨292.87 for just 1 messaging conversation.
  • Only 2 active campaigns: The majority of campaigns stopped delivering mid-period. Budget continuity should be reviewed to avoid gaps in reach.
  • Pending review campaign: New Leads Campaign Faisal Hills is in pending review — creative or targeting may need revision to pass Meta's approval.
  • Awareness #4 CPR is highest: While it generated the most reach (343K), the ₨20.71 CPR is the highest among awareness campaigns. Budget reallocation to lower-CPR awareness campaigns could improve efficiency.
6. Recommendations
  • Scale active engagement campaigns: Campaigns #7 and #8 (currently active) are performing at ₨0.052–₨0.083 CPR. Increase daily budget on these immediately.
  • Reactivate top engagement campaigns: Faisal Town 2 (₨0.074 CPR) and Eng. #6 (₨0.055 CPR) should be reactivated — they proved high efficiency when active.
  • Optimize lead campaigns: Replace broad messaging campaigns with more targeted lead forms. Test lookalike audiences from existing messaging contacts for better conversion rates.
  • Maintain awareness budget on low-CPR campaigns: Archived campaigns #2 (₨6.62) and #3 (₨5.07) showed the best reach efficiency — rebuild similar structures as active campaigns.
  • Review Leads Faisal Hills: Resolve the pending review issue and relaunch with updated creative to capture Faisal Hills leads.
  • Budget continuity: Avoid allowing all campaigns to go inactive simultaneously. Keep at least 4–5 campaigns running at all times for consistent brand presence.
